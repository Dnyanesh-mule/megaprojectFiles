GRAMPANCHAYAT SIREGAON BANDH

In Maharashtra, Gram Panchayats are governed by section 5 of the Bombay Gram Panchayat Act, 1958. Population is considered while establishing a new Gram Panchayat in the state. Gram Panchayat is managed with the help of Sarpanch, Upasarpanch, Gram Sevak. Gram Panchayat is the lowest but most important part of Panchayat Raj. There are 27,951 Gram Panchayats in Maharashtra. Sections in the Gram Panchayat Act As per Section 5 of the Maharashtra Gram Panchayat Act 1958, there should be one Gram Panchayat for every village. The people of the village shall elect their representatives in proportion to the population by direct voting as follows to carry out the functions of the Gram Panchayat. Voting of the said members shall be by adult and secret ballot.

Reservation

50% of the total seats are reserved for women in Gram Panchayat. Seats are reserved for Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes in proportion to population. 27% seats are reserved for people belonging to other backward class category.

Qualification of members

He should be a member of Gram Sabha of the village. His name must be in the electoral roll. He should have completed 21 years of age. Presidents of agricultural and credit cooperative societies can be taken as associate members. But it requires the permission of Gram Panchayat, now this method has been discontinued. Term – The term of Gram Panchayat is for 5 years. It is mandatory to hold elections within 6 months after dissolution of Gram Panchayat and no extension is allowed under any circumstances. The State Government has the right to dissolve the Gram Panchayat. If more than half of the members resign, the state government directs for by-elections or decides to dismiss. The Collector sends the report in that context to the Government. A population of 300 to 1500 in hilly areas has seven members. Sarpanch and Deputy Sarpanch Sarpanch is the executive head of Gram Panchayat. The members elected from Gram Panchayat elect Sarpanchs and Deputy Sarpanchs from among themselves. (Since 2017, election of Sarpanch is done directly from the people). The post of Sarpanch is reserved while the post of Sub-Sarpanch is open and the reservation draw is made in the Collector’s office before the election. The District Collector issues a notification to convene the first meeting of the Gram Panchayat after the election. An officer appointed by the District Collector presides over this meeting.

Gram sevak

Gram sevaka is known as Secretary, Chief Executive Officer or Chitnis of Gram Panchayat. The Gram Sevak is selected by the Zilla Parishad and appointed by the Chief Executive Officer. He is a servant of Zilla Parishad. His salary is from the district fund. He is closely controlled by the Group Development Officer.

Work of Gram Sevak

  • Preparation of Gram Panchayat budget.
  • To maintain the office of Gram Panchayat.
  • Advising village people on health, agriculture, village development, education etc.
  • To inform people about different government schemes for village development.
  • Collection of Taxes.
  • Issuance of various types of certificates to the public
  • To work as Registrar of Births, Deaths, Births and Deaths.
  • Acting as Child Marriage Prevention Officer.
  • Acting as Labor Registration Officer.
  • Registration of construction workers.
  • Acting Public Information Officer.
  • Acting as Biodiversity Committee Secretary.
  • To perform the functions of Registrar of Marriages.
  • Acting as Emergency Committee Secretary.
  • Planting trees, building nursery school and teaching to use it.
  • Acting as Secretary of Gram Sabha..
  • Income of Gram Panchayat
  • Tax on houses and vacant lots within Gram Panchayat limits
  • Business tax, travel tax, tax on purchase and sale of animals
  • Subsidy from State Government in proportion to land revenue
  • Grants from Zilla Parishad for development works.
  • Functions of Gram Panchayat
  • Construction of village roads.
  • To repair village roads.
  • Providing lighting.
  • Keeping records of births, deaths and marriages.
  • Maintaining public cleanliness.
  • Sewage management.
  • Supply of drinking water.
  • To provide education as well as health facilities.
  • Implementation of agricultural development and livestock improvement schemes.
  • To organize village bazaars, fairs, festivals, sandals, urus.
  • The taxes coming from the Gram Panchayat limits and the funds coming from the government should be used at appropriate places.

Works of Our Grampanchayat

  • To act as the Registrar of Births, Deaths, and Marriages.
  • Prevention of child marriage and enforcing related laws.
  • Registration of laborers and workers in the village.
  • Registration of construction workers.
  • Acting as Public Information Officer for village-related information.
  • Serving as Secretary of the Biodiversity Committee in the village.
  • Implementation of marriage registration functions.
  • Serving as the Secretary of the Emergency Committee.
  • Organizing tree plantation drives, building nursery schools, and promoting environmental awareness.
  • Serving as Secretary of the Gram Sabha (village assembly).
  • Tax collection within the Gram Panchayat jurisdiction, including property and business taxes.
  • Tax on houses, vacant lots, and commercial properties within the Gram Panchayat limits.
  • Business tax, travel tax, and tax on animal trade within the Gram Panchayat area.
  • Receiving subsidies from the State Government in proportion to land revenue collected.
  • Receiving grants from the Zilla Parishad (District Council) for village development works.
  • Construction and maintenance of village roads and pathways.
  • Repair and maintenance of existing village infrastructure and roads.
  • Providing public lighting systems and street lights.
  • Providing water supply to the village community.
  • Developing public sanitation facilities (e.g., toilets, drainage systems).
  • Organizing health and sanitation awareness campaigns for the villagers.
  • Supporting agricultural development, including irrigation and farming subsidies.
  • Setting up community centers, parks, and recreational facilities.
  • Promoting educational programs and providing scholarships for students.
  • Creating and implementing schemes for poverty alleviation and rural welfare.
